INCOGNITO: The Secret Lives of the Brain David Eagleman

Chapter: There’s Someone in My Head But It’s Not Me Published: 2011

Pgs. 1 - 8


  • Interlocking bones

  • Netting of sinewy muscles

  • Specialized fluid

  • Collaboration of internal organs chugging away to keep you alive

  • High tech self-healing sensory material – skin

  • Brain: 3 lbs of most complex material

    • Mission control center that drives entire operation

    • Built up of:

      • Cells called neurons and glia—each cell sends electrical pulses to other cells

    • As many connections in a single cubic centimeter of brain tissue as there are stars in the Milky Way galaxy

  • “The Machinery is utterly alien to us, and yet, somehow it is us”

  • The voice is Physical

    • If you build a little machine sensitive to detect tiny compressions of the molecules in the air, you can capture the density changes and reproduce them later (microphone)

  • What is thought?

    • Seems ephemeral and ineffable

    • Doesn’t have a shape or smell of any physical instanation

    • Underpinned by physical stuff

    • Alterations of brain change the kinds of thoughts we think

      • Deep sleep — no thoughts

      • Dream sleep — unbidden, bizarre thoughts

      • Day — normal, well-accepted thoughts

    • State of physical material determines state of the thoughts

    • Thoughts don’t have a physical basis, but they’re like feathers on the wind, depend on the integrity of the enigmatic three pound mission control center

  • Although we are dependent on the functioning of the brain for our inner lives, it runs its own show

  • Consciousness is like a tiny stowaway on a transatlantic steamship, taking credit for the journey without acknowledge g the massive engineering underfoot

  • Brains are in the business of gathering information and steering behavior appropriately

    • Doesn’t matter if consciousness is involved in the decision making; smallest player in the operations of the brain

  • Brain runs mostly on autopilot

  • Examples:

    • Hitting brakes

    • Noticing your name spoken in a conversation across the room that you weren’t listening to

    • Finding someone attractive without knowing why

  • Brain has been molded by evolutionary pressures, and so has consciousness

  • Activities that characterize a nation at any given moment:

    • Factories churn

    • Telecommunication lines buzz

    • Businesses ship products

    • Police chase criminals

    • Handshakes secure deals

    • Lovers rendezvous

    • Drivers navigate

  • Can’t take all the info at once, nor would it be useful

  • Conscious mind is the newspaper

  • Carl Jung: “In each of us, there is another whom we do not know.”
